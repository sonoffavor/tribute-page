Herbert E. Douglass

One of the most influential Adventist theologians of the 20th Century

Herbert Douglass pictured in 1973 while working at the Review and Herald  (now the Adventist Review).

Timeline of Douglass' Life

  • 1927 born in Springfield, Massachusetts
  • 1954-1957 served on the staff that edited the Seventh-day Adventist Bible Commentary
  • 1964 earned Doctorate in Theology at Pacific School of Religion
  • 1967-1970 served as president of Atlantic Union College
  • 1970-1976 served as editor of the Review and Herald magazine (now the Adventist Review)
  • 1979-1985 served as associate book editor and vice-president for editorial development at the Pacific Press Publishing Association
  • 1985-1992 served as president of Weimar Institute
  • 1997-2001 served as vice-president for philanthropy at Adventist Heritage ministry
  • 2003-2005 was a consultant for Amazing Facts ministry until his death in 2014

The Great Controversy Theme

The Great Controversy theme was a topic that Douglass enjoyed talking and writing about. He recognized that it is “the core concept that brings coherence to all Biblical subjects. It transcends the age-old divisions that have fractured the Christian church for centuries.”1 He articulates this theme in God at Risk and The Heartbeat of Adventism.

Theological Controvery

History testifies to the fact that theological controversies can be brutal. Douglass, however, never allowed theological differences to spoil a good friendship. The quote below is from a friend of Douglass who did not agree with him.

The more I knew about Herb, the more certain I was that he was wrong on certain theological issues, yet the more I liked and respected him. . . There are few scholars who can write for all audiences. Even rarer is the person who can have strong convictions, share them firmly, and yet do so in a cordial, even Christ-like way. That was my friend Herb Douglass.

-David Trim, Ph.D., F.R.Hist.S., is the Director of Archives, Statistics, and Research at the General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists.
